What Is React Native Used For?

Suppose you want to create a mobile app. A cross-platform one that works on Apple iPhones and Android phones. Of course, you want something quick, cheap, and reliable. That is where React Native could be beneficial. It is a famous framework that allows programmers to create applications directly on both of them by utilizing a single set of code. There is no necessity to create two different versions. It implies reduced efforts, fewer time requirements, and reduced expenditures. These benefits clearly explain why many businesses choose React Native for cross platform app development.

How Can You Hire React Native Developers in the UK?

The decision on how to Hire React Native Developers in the UK may sound like a big deal, but as long as there is a proper plan, it does not look as hard as one would think. In the UK, the React Native development staff is on the increase with their technical expertise and the agile mindset. In the case of a new product launch or enhancing an old one, the situation is easier when you go through the process with purpose and clarity:

1. Define Your Project Needs

The initial one is to learn precisely what you require. Is it a simple MVP to check out the demand on the market? A 100 per cent scalable product? Or are your existing app now equipped with a couple of new features? Well-defined objectives make communication easier, prevent scope creep, and pick developers with experience in the domain.

2. Pick the Right Hiring Style

There is no universal answer: the way you choose to hire depends on the schedule of the projects, the budget and the degree of engagement you are eager to have.

  • Freelancers: They are ideal for small jobs, building a prototype, or a quick fix. You will be required to control them, however.
  • In-house developers: Excellent when you want long-term products that require frequent updates and constant interaction.
  • Development agencies: Best to use when you have an immediate need to have a team to work under you: designers, developers, testers, all in one place, and you have concerns about speed and quality control.

3. Start Searching

Now, it’s time to find developers or teams.

For React Native developers, search:

  • LinkedIn – Look for experience, endorsements, and activity.
  • Upwork UK or Toptal is for pre-vetted talent with ratings and reviews.
  • GitHub – See how active they are and the quality of code they share.

Search Clutch, GoodFirms, or local agency directories if you’re looking for a company. Cities like London, Manchester, Bristol, and Leeds have strong developer communities with React Native expertise.

4. Check Their Previous Work

Don’t just take a resume or a website at face value. Ask for specific app links or case studies.

Here’s what to look for:

  • Are the apps live and performing well on app stores?
  • Do they have good reviews?
  • Does the design feel smooth and modern?
  • Have they solved real problems in past projects?

5. Take a Small Test

Before diving into a whole project, test the waters. Give them a minor assignment—maybe creating a login screen, integrating an API, or building a reusable component.

It reveals:

  • How they write code
  • Whether they follow best practices
  • How they handle feedback
  • How quickly and cleanly they deliver

It’s like dating before marriage—low risk, high value.

6. Talk It Out

Tech skills are essential, but communication is all that counts.

Zoom into a call or video conference. Ask:

  • What is your general response when doing a new project?
  • What is your communication and tracking software?
  • What is your process when it comes down to change requests or feedback?

Use this time to assess personality fit, too. Are the developers asking thoughtful questions? Are they excited about your idea?

7. Set Things Up and Kick Off

Once you’re ready to start, align everything:

  • Choose communication tools (Slack, Trello, Jira)
  • Define timelines and sprint goals
  • Decide how you’ll give and receive feedback

Most UK developers are familiar with Agile and flexible workflows. Weekly check-ins or sprint reviews help keep everyone on track and reduce surprises.

FAQs

Regarding the UK, hiring React native developers presents numerous choices, whether outsourcing, remote, or constructing in-house. To make the process a bit easier, the answers to these frequently asked questions are provided:

1. How do we outsource React Native Developers in the UK?

To outsource React Native development, contact software development firms already established in the UK. Vetted agencies can be identified using such websites as Clutch, GoodFirms, or LinkedIn. Ensure they possess a solid portfolio, Agile skills, and experience in the industry.

2. How to Hire Remote React Native Developers?

Find remote talent using Upwork UK, Toptal and Remote OK websites. You can also filter out UK-based developers who are open to remote positions via LinkedIn filters. One should always inquire about their background work and communication and their level of availability.

3. What Skills Should I Look for in a React Native Developer?

Find a skilled developer with React Native, JavaScript/TypeScript, Redux, and RESTful API who has worked in both iOS and Android deployments. It is also advantageous to be acquainted with Agile workflows, Git, and such tools as Expo.

4. What Is the Average Cost of Hiring a React Native Developer in the UK?

Costs vary by engagement model and experience. Freelancers may charge £25–£60/hour, while agencies can range from £300–£600/day. In-house salaries typically fall between £40,000–£70,000 per year.

5. Is It Better to Hire Freelancers or an Agency for a React Native App?

Freelancers are ideal for short-term tasks or MVPs on a tight budget. Agencies offer end-to-end support, better scalability, and team management—suitable for complete product development or time-sensitive projects.
